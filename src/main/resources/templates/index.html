Question 1: Multiple-Choice Questions

Various options are provided as possible answers to the following questions. Each question has only ONE correct answer. Choose the answer and select the letter (A–D) below each question.

  1. 1.1 Several forces are acting on a moving object. Which ONE of the following statements is CORRECT when these forces are in equilibrium?

    Correct Answer: B
    Approach: Forces in equilibrium mean the net force is zero (\( F_{\text{net}} = 0 \)). From Newton’s First Law (data sheet: \( F_{\text{net}} = ma \)), if \( F_{\text{net}} = 0 \), acceleration \( a = 0 \). For a moving object, this implies constant velocity.

  2. 1.2 A stone thrown vertically downwards from the top of a building takes t seconds to strike the ground. What does the shaded area between 0 and t seconds, shown in the acceleration-time graph, represent?

    Correct Answer: D
    Approach: The area under an acceleration-time graph equals the change in velocity (\( \Delta v = a \cdot \Delta t \)). In free fall, acceleration is constant (\( a = g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2 \), data sheet). The shaded area from 0 to \( t \) is \( g \cdot t \), representing \( \Delta v \).

  3. 1.3 A ball moving horizontally has constant momentum p and kinetic energy K. The ball collides with a wall and bounces back horizontally. Immediately after the collision, the ball has momentum ½p. The mass of the ball remains constant. Which ONE of the following is the kinetic energy of the ball immediately after the collision?

    Correct Answer: A
    Approach: Momentum \( p = mv \), kinetic energy \( K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \) (data sheet). If momentum is \( \frac{1}{2}p \), velocity is \( \frac{1}{2}v \). New kinetic energy: \( K' = \frac{1}{2}m\left(\frac{1}{2}v\right)^2 = \frac{1}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = \frac{1}{4}K \).

  4. 1.4 A force F acts on a box as the box moves from rest down a rough incline at a constant acceleration. The force is parallel to the incline.

    The work done by the gravitational force is ... the work done by the frictional force and the work done by F.

    Correct Answer: C
    Approach: Work \( W = F \Delta x \cos \theta \) (data sheet). Gravity’s work is positive (down incline). Friction’s work is negative (opposes motion). \( F \)’s work depends on direction. Net work equals kinetic energy change, making gravity’s work greater than the sum of friction and \( F \).

  5. 1.5 A ball falling vertically downwards from point A strikes the ground with velocity v and bounces, reaching a maximum height at point B. Which ONE of the following combinations is CORRECT for the direction of the impulse on the ball upon striking the ground and the magnitude of the velocity with which the ball leaves the ground?

    Direction of Impulse Magnitude of Velocity
    A Upward Greater than v
    B Downward Greater than v
    C Upward Less than v
    D Downward Less than v

    Correct Answer: C
    Approach: Impulse \( \Delta p = m(v_f - v_i) \) (data sheet). Initial velocity \( v_i = -v \) (down). After bouncing, velocity \( v_f \) is upward but smaller (lower height). Impulse is upward (changes direction). Magnitude \( |v_f| < v \).

  6. 1.6 The absorption spectrum of an element surrounding a moving star is observed on Earth and found to be red shifted. Which ONE of the following combinations is CORRECT for the movement of the star and the frequency of the observed light on Earth?

    Movement of Star Frequency of Observed Light
    A Away from Earth Decreased
    B Towards Earth Decreased
    C Away from Earth Increased
    D Towards Earth Increased

    Correct Answer: A
    Approach: Red shift increases wavelength, decreasing frequency (\( f = \frac{c}{\lambda} \), data sheet: \( c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s} \)). This occurs when the star moves away (Doppler effect).

  7. 1.7 Two small identical spheres, each with mass m and charge +Q, are placed in a vertical cylinder. The spheres remain stationary when their centres are r metres apart.

    Which ONE of the following expressions can be used to CORRECTLY calculate the distance r?

    Correct Answer: A
    Approach: Electrostatic force equals weight for equilibrium: \( \frac{kQ^2}{r^2} = mg \) (data sheet: \( k = 9.0 \times 10^9 \, \text{N·m}^2/\text{C}^2 \)). Solve: \( r^2 = \frac{kQ^2}{mg} \), so \( r = \sqrt{\frac{kQ^2}{mg}} \).

  8. 1.8 The kilowatt-hour (kWh) is a unit of ...

    Correct Answer: C
    Approach: Kilowatt-hour is energy (power × time). Power \( P = \frac{W}{\Delta t} \), work \( W = VIt \) (data sheet). Thus, kWh measures electrical energy.

  9. 1.9 The diagram below shows a simplified electric motor. The rotation of the coil is observed from the battery.

    Which ONE of the following statements is CORRECT while the motor is in operation?

    Correct Answer: D
    Approach: In a DC motor, the coil and commutator rotate together. Direction depends on current and magnetic field. Assuming standard setup (Fleming’s Left-Hand Rule), rotation is anti-clockwise from the battery view.

  10. 1.10 Which of the following statements is/are TRUE for the photoelectric effect? The photoelectric effect demonstrates that:

    (i) Light has a wave nature

    (ii) Light has a particle nature

    (iii) Light energy is quantised

    Correct Answer: D
    Approach: Photoelectric effect shows light as photons (particle nature) with quantized energy (\( E = hf \), data sheet: \( h = 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J·s} \)). Wave nature (i) is not demonstrated.

Question 2

In an experiment, a crate of mass 8,5 kg, lying stationary on a rough horizontal table, is connected to a mass hanger by means of a light inextensible string passing over a frictionless pulley. Mass pieces are added to the mass hanger and the acceleration of the crate is measured.

Ignore the effects of air friction.

  1. 2.1 Define the term static friction.

    Correct Answer: Static friction is the force that opposes the tendency of an object to move when an external force is applied, but the object remains stationary.
    Approach: Use the definition related to \( f_s^{\text{max}} = \mu_s N \) (data sheet). It acts to prevent motion until the applied force exceeds the maximum static friction.

  2. 2.2 Draw a labelled free-body diagram showing ALL the HORIZONTAL forces acting on the crate JUST BEFORE it starts moving.

    Correct Answer: Diagram shows tension (right) and static friction (left). Normal force (up) and weight (down) cancel vertically.
    Approach: Identify horizontal forces: tension from the string (right), static friction opposing motion (left). Use Newton’s First Law (\( F_{\text{net}} = 0 \)) before motion starts.

  3. 2.3 Calculate the:

    1. 2.3.1 Coefficient of static friction (μ_s)

      Correct Answer: [Requires graph data, e.g., \( \mu_s = 0.45 \)]
      Approach: Use \( f_s^{\text{max}} = \mu_s N \) (data sheet). Normal force \( N = mg = 8.5 \cdot 9.8 = 83.3 \, \text{N} \). Maximum static friction equals tension just before motion (from graph). Solve: \( \mu_s = \frac{f_s^{\text{max}}}{N} \).

    2. 2.3.2 Magnitude of the acceleration represented by Y on the graph if the coefficient of kinetic friction between the crate and the table is 0,40

      Correct Answer: [Requires graph data, e.g., \( a = 1.2 \, \text{m/s}^2 \)]
      Approach: Use Newton’s Second Law: \( F_{\text{net}} = ma \) (data sheet). Net force is tension minus kinetic friction (\( f_k = \mu_k N \), \( \mu_k = 0.4 \), \( N = 83.3 \, \text{N} \)). Solve for \( a = \frac{F_{\text{net}}}{m} \).

  4. 2.4 A 5 kg block is now placed inside the crate and the experiment is repeated. How will this affect the maximum static frictional force now experienced by the crate? Choose from INCREASES, DECREASES or REMAINS THE SAME.

    Correct Answer: A
    Reason: New mass is 13.5 kg, increasing normal force (\( N = mg \), data sheet). Maximum static friction \( f_s^{\text{max}} = \mu_s N \) increases due to larger \( N \).

Question 4

Ball P of mass 0,16 kg, moving east at a speed of 10 m·s-1, collides head-on with another ball Q of mass 0,2 kg, moving west at a speed of 15 m·s-1. After the collision, ball P moves west at a speed of 5 m·s-1, as shown in the diagram below.

Ignore the effects of friction and the rotational effects of the balls.

  1. 4.1 Define the term momentum in words.

    Correct Answer: Momentum is the product of an object’s mass and its velocity.
    Approach: Momentum is a vector quantity, \( p = mv \) (data sheet), representing the quantity of motion.

  2. 4.2 Calculate the:

    1. 4.2.1 Velocity of ball Q after the collision

      Correct Answer: -3.00 m/s (3.00 m/s west)

Substitute: \( (0.16 \times 10) + (0.2 \times -15) = (0.16 \times -5) + (0.2 \times v_{Q_f}) \). Solve: \( 1.6 - 3.0 = -0.8 + 0.2 v_{Q_f} \), so \( v_{Q_f} = -3.00 \, \text{m/s} \).

    2. 4.2.2 Magnitude of the impulse on ball P during the collision

      Correct Answer: 2.40 kg·m/s
      Approach: Impulse is the change in momentum: \( \Delta p = m_P (v_{P_f} - v_{P_i}) \) (data sheet). Substitute: \( \Delta p = 0.16 \times (-5 - 10) = 0.16 \times -15 = -2.4 \, \text{kg·m/s} \). Magnitude: \( 2.40 \, \text{kg·m/s} \).

Question 5

A roller-coaster car of mass 200 kg, with the engine switched off, travels along track ABC which has a rough surface, as shown in the diagram below. At point A, which is 10 m above the ground, the speed of the car is 4 m·s⁻¹.

At point B, which is at a height \( h \) above the ground, the speed of the car is 2 m·s⁻¹. During the motion from point A to point B, 3,40 × 10³ J of energy is used to overcome friction.

Ignore rotational effects due to the wheels of the car.

  1. 5.1 Define the term non-conservative force.

    Correct Answer: A non-conservative force is a force for which the work done depends on the path taken.
    Approach: Friction is non-conservative, as work varies with path (data sheet: \( W_{\text{nc}} = \Delta K + \Delta U \)).

  2. 5.2 Calculate the change in the kinetic energy of the car after it has travelled from point A to point B.

    Correct Answer: -1200 J
    Approach: \( \Delta K = K_f - K_i \), where \( K = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 \) (data sheet). Initial: \( K_i = \frac{1}{2} \times 200 \times 4^2 = 1600 \, \text{J} \). Final: \( K_f = \frac{1}{2} \times 200 \times 2^2 = 400 \, \text{J} \). Thus, \( \Delta K = 400 - 1600 = -1200 \, \text{J} \).

  3. 5.3 Use energy principles to calculate the height \( h \).

    Correct Answer: 9.30 m
    Approach: Use \( W_{\text{nc}} = \Delta K + \Delta U \). Friction work \( W_{\text{nc}} = -3400 \, \text{J} \). \( \Delta K = -1200 \, \text{J} \). Potential energy \( U = mgh \), so \( \Delta U = mg (h - 10) \). Substitute: \( -3400 = -1200 + 200 \times 9.8 \times (h - 10) \). Solve: \( h \approx 9.30 \, \text{m} \).

  4. 5.4 Calculate the power delivered by the engine to move the car from point B to point C.

    On reaching point B, the car's engine is switched on in order to move up the incline to point C, which is 22 m above the ground. While moving from point B to point C, the car travels for 15 s at a constant speed of 2 m·s⁻¹, while an average frictional force of 50 N acts on it.

    Correct Answer: 2626.67 W
    Approach: Power \( P = \frac{W}{\Delta t} \). Work \( W = W_g + W_f \). Gravitational work: \( W_g = mg \Delta h = 200 \times 9.8 \times (22 - 9.30) = 24892 \, \text{J} \). Friction work: \( W_f = F_f \Delta x \cos 180^\circ \), \( \Delta x = 2 \times 15 = 30 \, \text{m} \), so \( W_f = 50 \times 30 \times (-1) = -1500 \, \text{J} \). Total \( W = 24892 - 1500 = 23392 \, \text{J} \). Power: \( P = \frac{23392}{15} \approx 2626.67 \, \text{W} \).
